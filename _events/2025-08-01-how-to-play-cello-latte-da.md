NMTE member **Benjamin Emory Larson** premiered his musical *How to Play the Cello (Until You Don't)* as part of Theater Latte Da's prestigious **2025 NEXT Festival of New Musicals**.

Loosely based on the life of **Jacqueline du Pré**, one of the greatest cellists of all time, whose career was cut short in her 30s by Multiple Sclerosis. Through original songs and reimagined classical pieces, Jackie guides us on the explosive journey of her life — discovering the cello for the first time, falling in love with it, conquering the world, falling ill, and ultimately facing the question: *what is an artist who can't make art anymore?*

The piece runs 75 minutes with no intermission.

## From the Writer

> When theaters were shut down by COVID-19 in 2020, and it sank in that they wouldn't be reopening, I felt like I had wasted my life. I had poured everything I had into musical theater, and now it was gone, possibly for a long time. In 2024, my friend Anna Beth told me she had always wanted to play Jacqueline du Pré, and I jumped at the opportunity to write something for her, as well as explore the story of another artist who had lost their ability to make art.
>
> Writing about Jackie has healed me and challenged me and broken my heart in a hundred ways. I hope spending an evening with her does the same for you.
>
> PS. A cellist who can't play cello is: a cellist.
>
> — Benjamin Emory Larson

## About the NEXT Festival

Theater Latte Da's annual NEXT Festival is at the heart of their new work development initiatives. The 2025 festival featured three works — *The Homefront* (Sam Salmond, Malika Oyetimein & Jenny Stafford), *How to Play the Cello (Until You Don't)* (Benjamin Emory Larson), and *Okuni* (Naomi Iizuka & Paul Hodge) — with over 40 Twin Cities actors and nearly 1,200 audience members.

Also featured: NMTE member **Robert Elhai** in the festival's historical timeline of past productions, including orchestrations for *Johnny Skeeky* (2024) and participation in the 2020 NEXT UP and 2022 NEXT Festival programs. Member **Max Wojtanowicz** appeared in the 2018 NEXT Festival. Member **Bradley Greenwald** has been a regular collaborator across multiple Latte Da productions and festivals.
