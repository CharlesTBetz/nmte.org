NMTE member **Keith Benson** presented his original musical *Anzhelina & Karina* in a free public reading — a heart-warming story of two Russian cousins navigating love, loss, and redemption in 1919 New York City.

## Story

**New York City, 1919 — Lower East Side**

Cousins Anzhelina and Karina, inseparable as young children in Russia, reunite with great excitement after a 10-year separation. 

**Karina**, raised in a sheltered family on a South Dakota farm, holds childhood fantasies of this "glamorous" place, remembering her youthful voyage from Ellis Island to Grand Central Terminal.

**Anzhelina**, a field nurse in the Great War, sees through her war-torn eyes a "wicked" place filled with poverty, prejudice, and oppression. She carries guilt from having to decide who would live and die, leading to PTSD and a savior's complex.

Their opposing views quickly tear them apart. As they chart their paths and learn the truth about this often-perilous city, they also navigate challenges that could separate them forever — or bring them together again at last.

After witnessing street women in dire situations and a tragic event in New York history, they each take action to help. In the end, a cause greater than their differences brings them together to once again enjoy the love they shared as children.

## Development History

*Anzhelina & Karina* was developed through NMTE's collaborative process, including a workshop reading at Maureen's home in February 2025 before this public presentation.

The musical also featured in NMTE's 2026 Crooners Cabaret at Dunsmore Jazz Room, where **"With You"** was performed by Nicole Korbisch and Zakary Thomas Morton.
